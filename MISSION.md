# Mission: Title 19 eCFR Navigation for GTC Certification Students

## Why
GTC customers who bought certification courses need to learn how Title 19 of the CFR is organized so they can study customs regulations and answer exam-style navigation questions with confidence. The goal is to turn official eCFR structure and GTC examples into retrieval-friendly lessons and quizzes, while respecting that students cannot use eCFR during the exam.

## Success looks like
- Students can parse citations such as `19 CFR 111.28(b)` into title, part, section, and paragraph.
- Students can recognize high-value Title 19 parts by topic, including Customs Brokers, Modernized Drawback, Entry of Merchandise, Entry Process, Mail Importations, and Packing/Marking.
- Students can answer multiple-choice CFR navigation questions from memory or permitted study materials, not from live eCFR search.
- Quiz feedback teaches the navigation logic, not just the correct letter.
- Every quiz key is backed by official eCFR or other government-source evidence.

## Constraints
- Audience: GTC customers preparing for certifications after buying GTC courses.
- Use official government sources for regulatory facts, especially eCFR Title 19.
- Treat eCFR digital navigation as a study tool only; do not teach it as an exam-time strategy.
- Use the existing `source-inputs/` seed data and exam screenshot as examples, not as the whole course.
- Keep lessons practical, exam-focused, and plain-language.
- Quizzes should build durable recall through immediate feedback, retrieval practice, and answer choices that avoid giving away the answer by length or formatting.

## Out of scope
- Legal advice or interpretation beyond training students to navigate and identify CFR structure.
- Broad import/export compliance instruction unrelated to Title 19 navigation.
- Relying on AI-generated summaries without official-source verification.
- Building a full course platform before the first quiz format is proven.
